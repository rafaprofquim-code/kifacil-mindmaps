Time Capsule — 1911
Rutherford: "Before we begin, let's recall Thomson's idea. According to him, the atom would be a sphere of positive charge spread throughout its whole volume, containing electrons distributed within it, like raisins in a pudding. For a while, this model seemed to explain well what scientists observed. But, like every good scientist, we should not accept an idea just because it seems correct. Let's put it to the test. Bring me the polonium source, inside the lead box."

The atomic nucleus has been discovered!

You helped Rutherford prove that Thomson's atom was incomplete. The positive charge is not spread throughout the whole volume — it is concentrated in a small, dense nucleus that holds almost all the atom's mass. Around it, an immense empty space where the electrons move. This is the planetary model: nucleus at the center, electrosphere around it, like planets orbiting the Sun.
